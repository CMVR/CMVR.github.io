Co-designing A Customisable VR Sensory Room with Autistic Adults

Participatory research developing personalised virtual sensory environments

PCVR · Co-design Research · Unity / C#

This project explores the co-design of customisable virtual reality sensory rooms with autistic adults. Working directly with participants, I developed a VR environment that allows users to personalise sensory elements to create calming, comfortable spaces tailored to their individual preferences.

The research investigates how VR can provide autistic individuals with a portable, accessible solution for sensory regulation, while the co-design approach ensures the technology genuinely meets user needs.

Gallery

Key Features

The VR sensory room includes extensive customisation options developed through iterative co-design sessions with autistic adults:

  • Lighting Settings — Adjustable brightness, colour temperature, and ambient lighting
  • Room Colours — Customisable colour schemes to suit individual preferences
  • Room Features — Toggleable environmental elements and objects
  • Passthrough Settings — Mixed reality options for grounding
  • Outside Settings — Natural environments including forests and water features

Technical Details

Built in Unity using C#, the prototype was developed for PCVR platforms to leverage high-fidelity graphics during the co-design research phase. The environment features procedurally generated natural landscapes, dynamic lighting systems, and an intuitive menu system for real-time customisation.

Related Publication

This project informed the research published in Insights into Sensory and Relaxation Preferences to Inform the Design of Calming Spaces and Sensory Rooms for Autistic Adults (Autism in Adulthood, 2025).
